## 8. The Cores

Each owns one primitive; the contract (§7) unifies them. Shape tracks sphere only as a consequence of grouping like primitives, never as a rule the storage layer enforces (§6.1). Cores are not sphere-locked (§6.2), and the homes named below are the reference tree's (§2) — an illustration, never a shape the tools impose. What lands a record at one of them is the constitution, which governs your tree identically.

### 8.1 Album — *Societas* agents (who)
People and agents. **Partitioned**; filename kinds `person` (an individual), `organization` (a formal body — a company, a school, a state), `group` (an informal set of people — a family, a friend group, a book club) — `csa__person__john_appleseed.json`, or `csa_john_appleseed__person.json` when a person is promoted to their own node (slug = the node's definition, §5.1–§5.2). Homed under Societas (`csng` Gens, `csnf` Familia, `csa` Amicitia, `cso` Officium, `csc` Civitas), filed by the **nature of the bond** — one person, one file. Closeness, role, origin, and gender are **fields, not nodes** (§2): the kind says *what* an agent is — person, organization, group — and is corrected only by the file-rename `edit -k` (§7.2), whereas a form of address like *Mr/Ms* is **derived** from the gender `data` field — corrected in place like any entity field (I1) — never encoded in the kind. *Where you met them* is provenance and *which context they belong to* is an edge; neither is ever their home (I3, I9). A family, a club, or a company is itself an entity, and a person's **membership is a reference** to it (`refs: ["album:book_club"]`) — an edge read from either end, never a nesting; you file the person once and link, rather than copying them under each group. (This is why there are no node references, §5.4, §18 — you point at the group *entity*, not a directory.) A person's availability is a list field (`data.away`, §6.1) that a lens overlays on a timeline by reading Album's JSON (§8.4, §12). Referenced everywhere as `album:<slug>`.

### 8.2 Mappa — *Locus* places (where)
In the reference tree, homed under `cl*` (Habitat, Statio, Urbs, Orbis, Rete). **Partitioned**; kinds `location` (an addressable point — a house, a café, a virtual room) and `region` (an area with an extent — a forest you own, a district), each entity one `.json` carrying coordinates / address / URL / bounds / timezone in `data`. The split is point-vs-area, a real `data`-shape difference, **not** small-vs-large: scale is the tree's cut (Habitat → Orbis), and a kind vocabulary that re-cut it would only duplicate the tree in a field. Mappa holds *places*, not your history among them: **where you've been** is an Annales `log` line referencing `mappa:<place>` (a dated fact about your movement — aboutness, §8.6), and **where you live** is a Fasti residence `span` referencing a `location` (§8.4) — residence is your *relationship* to a place, a period rather than a place-type, so it is never a Mappa kind. Referenced as `mappa:<slug>`.

### 8.3 Rationes — *Res* holdings (what)
Holdings, **partitioned**; kinds `account`, `asset` (a good), `claim` (a right, licence, subscription, or debt owed to you). The kind determines whether the holding carries a **balance series** — Rationes' one `Series` token, declared `Series { named: false }` (§7.1): `account` and `asset` may have `[code]__balance__<slug>.jsonl`, one keyed line per date (a changing balance or valuation); `claim` has none (an expiry date is a field). That series is **determined, not named** — the `named: false` the spine reads: its name slot carries its holding's slug, so it is minted by the holding's creation rather than by `-c`, has nothing to mistype (§7.3), is reached through the holding rather than referenced on its own (§5.4), and moves with it (§7.2). `rat series checking` returns the balance trend, and net worth folds the latest balance of the kinds that *have* one, never stored (I1) — your passport (`claim`) isn't part of it. Homed under Res (`crb` Bona, `crp` Pecunia, `cri` Iura), filed by what the holding *is*, but **not sphere-locked** (§6.2): where you model an org as its own node, *its* account is the org's stuff and lives there (`…__account__bank.json`), and the tree-walking fold still sums it into net worth wherever it sits — while your own holdings home under Res regardless of which org they concern, the org tie a reference (`refs: ["album:<org>"]`). Referenced as `rationes:<slug>`.

### 8.4 Fasti — time / events (placement)
The **placement** tense of Actio: what sits on the timeline. Two record shapes, Rationes-fashion (§8.3):

- **`span`** — a **Partitioned entity**, one `.json` per span: a real *period or state* with a `from` and an (optionally open) `to` — a life, a project's active window, a career stage, an enrolment, a residence. Long-lived, mutable (close the `to` when it ends), and heavily referenced, so it earns entity storage (§6.1); referenced as `fasti:<slug>` and resolved by filename without opening it (§5.0). A span references what it concerns — a career span `refs: ["album:<org>"]`, a residence span `refs: ["mappa:<place>"]`, a friend's residence both (`refs: ["album:alex", "mappa:hong_kong"]`) — the same edge everywhere (I9).
- **`event`** — a **`Series` token**, hand-named (`Series { named: true }`, §7.1) and so referenceable as a collection (`fasti:standups`, §5.4): one keyed line per dated occurrence (a meeting 4–5pm, a deadline). An event **optionally references the span it belongs to** (`refs: ["fasti:mvp_phase"]`), and may point at any other `core:slug` target — a task, a log, a note (§5.4). A calendar is an arbitrary surface and a span a real period, so an event points at its period, never at a container (I9). There is no `cal` token.

**The calendar is a derived view, never stored** — a fold over events by span and date. Album's `data.away` overlay is a **lens's** composition, not Fasti's: a lens reads both cores' JSON off `PATH` (I4, §12) and derives the overlap at render, so nothing is copied into Fasti (I1) and no core reads another (I5); where Album isn't installed the overlay is simply absent. An event with **no span is legal**, not a validation finding and not a stored flag; you surface the set on demand with `fas list --unspanned` — a derived filter over the same fold, so you can check them without anything nagging. *Motus* (the world's own doing) is deliberately empty (§2): an external occurrence enters as a sample — a Fasti event when you place it on the timeline, an Annales log when you record that it happened (§8.6).

### 8.5 Pensum — tasks (intention)
The **intention** tense: a future doing. A **series** — the node's tasks as keyed lines in `[code]__task.jsonl` (nameless: one task series per node, minted by the node's first task rather than by `-c`, §7.3), keyed by the task's own name (§5.4) — renaming it re-slugs and cascades its refs (§7.2) — edited in place (mark done, rename). Auspex's primary write target (§9), and the series a detached hook is likeliest to write while a hand is mid-edit — hence the file lock (§6.4). A task is homed where the doing lives (e.g. `acm`) and references what it is about (`refs: ["album:alex"]`).

### 8.6 Annales — logs (fact)
The **fact** tense: what happened — the purest expression of I1. A **series** (`cso__log__meetings.jsonl`), one file per log, one keyed line per fact; a correction rewrites the keyed line in place (§6.1). The record is the truth, never an audit sink — a fact is a fact whoever wrote it, and no line carries its author (§9.5). Where a rule keeps a memory of its own runs — a debounce, a note of what it proposed — it keeps it **here and nowhere else**, as an ordinary log it proposes like any other: a fact you chose to keep, not a sink the system maintains (§9.4–§9.5). Retrospective accounting lives here, not in Fasti: **time spent** (a `log` of what you gave your hours to), **where you've been** (a `log` line referencing `mappa:<place>`, §8.2) — both are dated facts, past-only, distinct from a Fasti event, which *places* something on the timeline and may be future. The same wall-clock hour can yield both a Fasti event (*a meeting sits at 4–5pm*) and an Annales log (*I spent 4–5pm on it*): different aboutness, different records.

A log is hand-named, so it is **minted explicitly** — `ann ecv weight -c` — and thereafter a plain `ann` appends a reading under whichever of §7.3's four forms names it, the tool listing candidates and refusing rather than guessing wherever more than one series answers.

*(Fasti, Pensum, and Annales are the three tenses of Actio — placement, intention, fact. See [Appendix A](./APPENDIX-A-NAMES.md).)*

### 8.7 Tabella — documents (meaning)
Captured meaning: notes, quotes, principles, reflections. Ego's **Document** shape (§6.1): one loose text file per document (`[code]_[slug].md`, also `.txt`/`.mdx`), edited in place. The body is opaque prose — an image or sketch that *is* the thought rides beside it as bulk (§6.5); the `+++` TOML frontmatter carries `type` and `tags` only, and is all a fold reads. **`type` is the note-kind** — a quote (received), a principle (normative), a reflection (interpretive), a portrayal (descriptive), or any the user defines — but a frontmatter *field*, never a `kinds()` token: Tabella declares none (§7.1), which is why its filenames carry no `__` segment. A note homes at what it is *about*, not the activity that spawned it (I3, §2): interview notes home **at the interviewee's node** — the case that promotes a person to an entity-as-node (§5.1), since a home is a node and a note about someone must land *in* them, not point at them (I3, §5.4) — a principle in Anima (`eam`/`eav`/`ear`), a reflection on a project at that project. The self is the hardest primitive to partition, so Ego is served by one core for now. Referenced as `tabella:<slug>` (§5.4) — its slug its name, soft-warned on duplicates like any core's. `get` returns frontmatter + body, `list` the frontmatter fold (§7.2); Tabella declares no read flags of its own, so selecting on `type` or `tags` is the caller's, over the emitted JSON (I4).
